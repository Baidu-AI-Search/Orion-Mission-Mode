## Decisions made

1. **Corporate event sponsorships are assigned by GTM team:** Platform → re:Invent, CI/CD → Google Next, GitOps → KubeCon. Each sponsoring PMM owns driving campaign manager commitment for their event.
2. **Commit product announcements will take a "matured feature" approach:** rather than announcing individual small MVCs, we'll bundle capabilities shipped over the past ~12 months into themed buckets (e.g., UX improvements, vulnerability management) — similar to how we framed the 14.0 launch — with a target of ~3 items per stage and a **top 5 overall** to feed PR and the product/Sid keynotes.
3. **14.0 content is fair game to reuse** for Commit announcements, and excitement ratings (1–3) will be treated as a stack rank (only one "3" per PMM) rather than an absolute score.
4. **Competitive comparison sheet — scope & style locked for v1:** tier 1 competitors only (ADO, Atlassian, GitHub, Jenkins, JFrog, CloudBees); include only competitors relevant to each stage; add a GitLab row; keep the green-only palette (no red/yellow "shaming") to keep it an industry-comparison asset rather than attack marketing; explicitly call out platform vendors that score 0 on a stage.
5. **Messaging framework pillars (working draft):** *"A single source of truth. Countless possibilities."* (transparency/SSOT), *"End-to-end control over your software factory"* (the all-in-one platform), and *"More speed, less risk"* (velocity + security/quality).
