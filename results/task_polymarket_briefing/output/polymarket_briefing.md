## 3. 🏦 Fed July 2026 Rate Decision — Will the Fed Cut, Hold, or Hike?

**Why it's hot:** After a string of hot inflation prints that had markets debating a possible **rate hike**, the June CPI print released the evening of July 14 came in dramatically below expectations, instantly reshaping every macro market on Polymarket (including rate cuts, BTC price, and recession odds).

**Market state before the CPI print:**
- Polymarket was pricing roughly a **40%+ probability of a July rate hike** following hawkish FOMC minutes and two dissents (Kashkari, Hammack) arguing the Fed should remove its easing bias.
- The "Fed cuts rates zero times in 2026" contract was trading around **78 cents**.

**The catalyst (July 14 at 8:30 PM Beijing / 8:30 AM ET):**
- **Headline CPI: −0.4% month-over-month** (vs. −0.2% expected), the largest monthly drop since April 2020; YoY fell to **3.5%** from 4.2% in May.
- **Core CPI: 0.0% MoM** (vs. +0.2% expected), YoY **2.6%** vs. 2.9% expected.
- Gasoline prices fell ~10%; auto insurance posted a large drop; lodging saw its biggest decline in over a year.
- Post-print: S&P futures ripped higher, 2-year Treasury yield dropped ~14 bps to ~4.14% (biggest one-day move since February), gold/silver jumped ~2%, dollar index dumped.
- Swap markets **slashed July hike probability from ~40%+ to ~20%**. On Polymarket, the "July hold" contract spiked and the "2026 zero cuts" contract sold off hard as traders priced in a better chance of a late-2026 cut.

**Why people are still debating (the bull/bear case):**
- **Bulls (cut case):** The Iran-war energy shock is clearly fading; oil is ~30% off its wartime peak; Goldman's July 12 note already argued the commodity impulse would fade through Q3-Q4. June's data validates that.
- **Bears (hold/hike case):** New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh testified on July 14 that the Fed has "zero tolerance" for above-target inflation; two FOMC dissenters want the statement to explicitly put rate hikes back on the table; and recent US-Iran naval clashes in the Strait of Hormuz could send oil back toward $100, reigniting inflation.

**Sources:** WeChat/ChinaFundNews CPI reaction (July 14), Goldman Sachs July 12 note (163.com), Kashkari/Hammack dissent reports (GitHub finance digest, July 15), CME FedWatch referenced via 163.com (July 15).
