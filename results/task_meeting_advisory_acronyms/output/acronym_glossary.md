# Acronym Glossary — CSMAC Meeting, May 30, 2012

This glossary catalogs the acronyms and abbreviations used in the transcript of the Commerce Spectrum Management Advisory Committee (CSMAC) meeting held on May 30, 2012, which focused on federal-commercial spectrum sharing, particularly in the 1695–1710 MHz and 1755–1850 MHz bands.

| Acronym | Full Form | Context | Category |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------|
| AWS | Advanced Wireless Services | Reference to the AWS-1 (1710–1755 / 2110–2155 MHz) band experience when discussing the guidance document (TSB 10-F) used for sharing between federal systems and emerging commercial mobile services in that band. | Spectrum/Technical |
| CMRS | Commercial Mobile Radio Service | Mentioned by Mr. Tramont when noting that individual CMRS providers (wireless carriers) will have different deployment models from unlicensed providers and manufacturers, complicating uniform assumptions across working groups. | Regulatory |
| CMR | Charles M. Rush (CMR Consulting) | Dr. Charles Rush’s consulting practice ("CMR Consulting"); he self-identifies affiliation as "CMR Consulting" during roll-call. | Industry |
| CSEA | Commercial Spectrum Enhancement Act | Referenced by Mr. Nebbia as the formal statutory process under which relocation cost estimates for federal systems are firmed up (compared against 2001 estimates for the 1710–1755 MHz relocation). | Regulatory |
| CSMAC | Commerce Spectrum Management Advisory Committee | The advisory body holding the meeting; formed under the Department of Commerce to advise NTIA on spectrum policy. The entire discussion centers on CSMAC’s new working-group effort on federal-commercial sharing. | Government Agency |
| CTIA | Cellular Telecommunications Industry Association (now "CTIA – The Wireless Association") | Industry trade group working with T-Mobile on a Special Temporary Authority (STA) request to conduct measurements in the 1755–1850 MHz band, and historically the body that coordinated industry parameter inputs for IMT-2000 sharing studies. | Industry |
| D.C. | District of Columbia | Location of the meeting (Herbert C. Hoover Building, 1401 Constitution Avenue, N.W., Washington, D.C.). | Other |
| DHS | Department of Homeland Security | Cited as one of the federal agencies (along with the Department of Justice) that NTIA is seeking to nominate a government co-chair for the law-enforcement surveillance working group. | Government Agency |
| DoD | Department of Defense | Major federal spectrum user in the 1755–1850 MHz band, operating satellite control links, electronic warfare training ranges, tactical radio relay, and airborne systems; John Hunter (detailed from DoD via T-Mobile) leads the airborne-operations work. | Military |
| EOD | Explosive Ordnance Disposal (referred to as "electronic ordinance disposal") | Listed by Mr. Nebbia as one of the short-distance federal law-enforcement / public-safety links in 1755–1850 MHz alongside surveillance systems, to be studied by Working Group 2. | Military |
| FCC | Federal Communications Commission | Independent regulator that manages non-federal spectrum; referenced repeatedly as the body that will conduct its own rulemaking/auction process once NTIA/CSMAC make sharing recommendations, and as an active participant in each working group. | Government Agency |
| FTI | Freedom Technologies, Inc. | Janice Obuchowski’s consulting firm, which she cites during self-introduction ("Janice Obuchowski at FTI"). | Industry |
| GHz | Gigahertz | Unit of frequency; used when discussing the 5 GHz unlicensed band and NTIA’s addition of 195 MHz in the 5 GHz range to the ten-year spectrum study plan. | Spectrum/Technical |
| IMT-2000 (INT2000) | International Mobile Telecommunications-2000 | Third-generation (3G) mobile standard; Member Rush recalls the year-2000 FCC/industry parameter-collection effort that produced the ITU-R sharing parameters for IMT-2000, which he cites as a precedent for this CSMAC effort. | Spectrum/Technical |
| IMT-Advanced (INT Advance) | International Mobile Telecommunications-Advanced | Fourth-generation (4G) mobile standard follow-on to IMT-2000; Mr. Rush notes ITU-R is currently developing parameters for IMT-Advanced but says CSMAC cannot wait for that process. | Spectrum/Technical |
| IP | Intellectual Property | Mr. Power notes that on the commercial side, proprietary plans and IP are analogous to classified information on the government side, creating disclosure sensitivities in joint discussions. | Industry |
| ISART | International Symposium on Advanced Radio Technologies | Annual spectrum-sharing conference hosted by NTIA’s ITS in Boulder, Colorado; Mr. Nebbia notes ISART will be held the two days immediately after the July 24 CSMAC meeting and will focus on spectrum sharing. | Industry |
| ITS | Institute for Telecommunication Sciences | NTIA’s research and engineering laboratory in Boulder, Colorado, which hosts ISART; referenced as the venue/laboratory anchor for the Boulder meeting week. | Government Agency |
| ITU-R | International Telecommunication Union – Radiocommunication Sector | UN body that develops global radio regulations and sharing parameters; Mr. Rush notes the IMT-2000 parameters were submitted to ITU-R and that ITU-R is now working on IMT-Advanced parameters. | Regulatory |
| LLP | Limited Liability Partnership | Suffix used in the names of two law firms represented on the committee: Wiley Rein LLP and Wilkinson Barker Knauer LLP. | Other |
| LTE | Long Term Evolution | Identified by Member Rush as the dominant 4G commercial standards family that the working groups should assume for future deployments in the studied bands, making parameter convergence practical. | Spectrum/Technical |
| LLC | Limited Liability Company | Legal-entity suffix used by several organizations noted on the attendee list (e.g., Lockheed Martin Corporation, Exelon Corporation — appears in member affiliations). | Other |
| MHz | Megahertz | Unit of frequency used throughout the meeting to describe bands (1695–1710, 1710–1755, 1755–1850, 2025–2110, 2155–2180, 3550/3650), the 500 MHz presidential goal, and bandwidth allocations. | Spectrum/Technical |
| NFL | National Football League | Used colloquially ("NFL cities") to refer to the largest U.S. metropolitan markets where commercial carriers most want early access during band relocation. | Other |
| NOAA | National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration | Parent agency of the National Weather Service; Yvonne Navarro of NOAA/Commerce is the recommended government co-chair for Working Group 1, which addresses weather-satellite receivers in the 1695–1710 MHz band. | Government Agency |
| N.W. | Northwest (quadrant of Washington, D.C.) | Street quadrant in the meeting address ("1401 Constitution Avenue, N.W."). | Other |
| NTIA | National Telecommunications and Information Administration | Commerce Department agency responsible for managing federal spectrum; Karl Nebbia and Larry Strickling are NTIA officials leading the 1755–1850 MHz report and the CSMAC working-group process. | Government Agency |
| OMB | Office of Management and Budget | White House budget office that agencies engaged to link relocation cost estimates to potential moves into comparable bands during the 1755–1850 MHz study. | Government Agency |
| OSTP | Office of Science and Technology Policy (White House) | Tom Power represents OSTP (referred to as "White House Office of Science and Technology Policy"); he delivered opening remarks reinforcing the 500 MHz/ten-year presidential goal and the sharing imperative. | Government Agency |
| PCAST | President’s Council of Advisors on Science and Technology | Referenced by Ms. Obuchowski as the source of a recent spectrum study whose recommendations (including shifting commission functions to NTIA) differ from a traditional carrier-exclusive-licensing view, creating uncertainty about FCC direction. | Government Agency |
| PCS | Personal Communications Services | Cited by Mr. Gibson as a 1990s-era sharing precedent (PCS vs. microwave systems), used as a historical parallel when discussing how sharing paradigms evolved for AWS. | Spectrum/Technical |
| STA | Special Temporary Authority | Type of FCC experimental license; T-Mobile, in cooperation with CTIA, has filed an STA request to do real-world measurements and testing in the 1755–1850 MHz band to assess interference with federal airborne operations. | Regulatory |
| TIA | Telecommunications Industry Association | Standards-development organization that maintains sharing-guidance documents such as TSB 10-F; Mr. Gibson urges that TIA and similar bodies be included so their guidance documents can be updated based on the CSMAC work. | Industry |
| TMI | Too Much Information | Colloquial interjection by Tom Power after his sharing-with-your-brother analogy. | Other |
| TSB 10-F | Telecommunications Systems Bulletin 10-F | TIA Interference Criteria bulletin referenced as the "guidance document" that enabled successful sharing analyses for the AWS band between federal systems and commercial wireless; cited as a precedent for producing updated guidance documents from this CSMAC effort. | Spectrum/Technical |
| TV | Television | Referenced in "TV White Spaces" — the FCC program enabling unlicensed devices to use unused TV broadcast spectrum, cited by Mr. Power as one model of spectrum sharing. | Spectrum/Technical |
| UAV | Unmanned Aerial Vehicle | Airborne federal systems (along with precision-guided munitions, air-combat training systems, and telemetry) operating in 1755–1850 MHz; cited as one of the most challenging federal uses to relocate or share with because of their intermittent, wide-area, high-altitude operation. | Military |
| Wi-Fi | Wireless Fidelity (IEEE 802.11) | Used as a precedent for successful government-industry-Commission cooperation on sharing in the 5 GHz band; cited by Mr. Nebbia as a model for how the new CSMAC working groups can produce consensus outcomes. | Spectrum/Technical |
| WRC-95 | World Radiocommunication Conference 1995 | ITU treaty conference referenced anecdotally by Chair Fontes, who recalls U.S. positions drawing criticism from other countries; he uses it to frame the present CSMAC effort as a "roll-up-your-sleeves" problem-solving opportunity. | Regulatory |

## Notes on methodology
- Acronyms that were explicitly expanded in the transcript (e.g., CSMAC, NTIA, FCC, CTIA, DoD, DHS, NOAA, OMB, OSTP, ITU-R, LTE, UAV, Wi-Fi, TV, PCS, AWS, CMRS, TIA, PCAST, STA, MHz, GHz, CSEA, ISART, ITS, IMT-2000, TSB 10-F) are listed as used in the meeting.
- Acronyms and shorthand that are purely domain terminology (e.g., INT2000/INT Advance for IMT-2000/IMT-Advanced, as pronounced in the colloquial back-and-forth) are cross-referenced to their standard full forms.
- Firm-name shorthand used in attendee introductions (CMR, FTI, LLP/LLC, N.W., D.C.) and colloquialisms (TMI, NFL) are included for completeness because the user asked to capture all abbreviations appearing in the transcript, including one-off uses.
