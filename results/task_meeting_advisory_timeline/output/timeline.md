## 2. Working Group Deadlines

Five working groups are being stood up. Two existing subcommittees — the **500 MHz Existing Subcommittee** and the **Sharing Subcommittee** — will be **put on hiatus ("stood down")** while this work proceeds so members can focus. The remaining existing committees (e.g., Spectrum Management Improvements on License) will assess their workplans within ~10 days and decide what can wrap up by July/September and what continues.

All working groups will be co-chaired by one industry representative and one government representative, with NTIA and FCC providing participants; outputs must be **consensus** recommendations routed through CSMAC to NTIA.

| Working Group | Scope / Focus | Government Co-Chair (identified or pending) | Target Deadline | Notes / Conditions |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **WG1 — 1695–1710 MHz (Weather Satellite Receivers)** | Re-evaluate/refine exclusion/coordination areas around weather satellite receivers to see if they can be shrunk or improved over the fast-track assumptions. | Yvonne Navarro (Commerce/NOAA) recommended; Ed Drocella (NTIA) participating. | **September 2012** (earliest target) | Kick off as quickly as possible; issues are considered more limited/straightforward than other bands. |
| **WG2 — Law Enforcement Surveillance, EOD, and Short-Distance Links (in 1755–1850)** | Examine transition plan for federal low-power law-enforcement systems; align city-by-city rollout order (starting with "NFL cities") with commercial buildout needs. | DHS/Justice (one name received, to be confirmed); Rich Orsulak and Scott Jackson (NTIA). | **January 2013** (primary target) | Must account for 3-step agency plan: (1) vacate 1755–1780 within 5 years going digital, (2) possibly squeeze to smaller MHz later, (3) eventually possibly exit the band. |
| **WG3 — Satellite Control & Electronic Warfare (in 1755–1850)** | Develop regulatory construct to protect satellite control uplinks and DoD EW training ranges while enabling commercial access; DoD band of greatest interest for potential relocation is 2025–2110 MHz. | Government co-chair to be named. | **January 2013** (primary target) | Satellites will not move in the near term; EW requires training guarantees. Interference concerns flow *into* commercial systems in this case. Moving federal systems into 2025–2110 MHz may trigger another FCC rulemaking. |
| **WG4 — Tactical Radio Relay & Fixed Microwave (in 1755–1850)** | Narrow exclusion areas around the three permanent tactical radio relay sites; build on 1710–1755 fixed-microwave relocation experience; explore military movement toward commercial-type technologies. | Gary Patrick (veteran of 1710–1755 relocation) involved. | **January 2013** (primary target) | Past exclusion zones shrank substantially once industry tailored deployments around sites; optimism that protected areas can be smaller than initial models suggest. |
| **WG5 — Airborne Operations (UAVs, precision-guided munitions, air combat training, telemetry) (in 1755–1850)** | Characterize airborne emitters (bandwidth, timing, duty cycle, distance); use T-Mobile/CTIA STA measurements; determine what industry can tolerate; identify sharing approaches. | John Hunter (on loan to NTIA from DoD, ex-T-Mobile) assigned. | **January 2013** (primary target) | Characterized as the "biggest challenge." Expected to take longer than microwave relocation; outcomes will depend heavily on real-world measurements. |

**Cross-cutting process deadlines:**
- Within ~1 week of May 30, 2012: send out participant invitations, post Bruce Washington's contact for volunteers, firm up co-chairs and CSMAC liaisons.
- Within ~10 days of May 30, 2012: existing (non-hiatus) committee co-chairs report back on what they will wrap up by July vs. keep going; Karl's team flags any items needed by September.
- July 24, 2012 (Boulder) and September 2012 CSMAC meetings: iterative updates from all WGs.
- January 2013: final consensus reports for most groups, to feed FCC action in time to pair with 2155–2180 MHz.

## 4. Historical References (Past Events Providing Context)

- **c. Late 2009 (~2.5 years prior):** Obama administration set the 500 MHz / 10-year wireless-broadband re-purposing goal. (Tom Power)
- **June 2010 (~2 years prior):** Presidential memorandum codifying the 500 MHz / 10-year goal. (Tom Power)
- **~December 2010 (within 6 months of the memo):** NTIA's "fast track" report identified 115 MHz (1695–1710 MHz and 3550–3650 MHz) for wireless broadband using exclusion areas; launched a ten-year plan covering 2,200+ MHz of government spectrum. (Tom Power / Karl Nebbia)
- **Pre-2012:** Agencies had previously **yielded the 1710–1755 MHz band**; that relocation (predominantly microwave systems) was completed in **5 years** and is used as a comparison point. (Karl Nebbia)
- **c. 2001:** Initial CSEA cost estimates for 1710–1755 MHz were ~$900 million; actual cost-to-date ~$1.5 billion — cited to argue cost estimates mature through the formal CSEA process. (Karl Nebbia)
- **October 1, 2011:** NTIA 1755–1850 MHz report date ("infamous October 1st of last year"); the report found over 3,100 assignments across 8+ applications and an estimated ~$18 billion relocation cost over 10 years. (Karl Nebbia)
- **5 GHz Wi-Fi sharing effort (past):** Held up by NTIA as a successful model of industry/government/FCC cooperative technical work producing international consensus outcomes. (Karl Nebbia)
- **Year 2000 / INT2000:** Charlie Rush recounted his FCC work with Diane Cornell and CTIA that produced sharing parameters that became ITU-R documentation — suggested as a process model. (Charlie Rush)
- **WRC-95 (1995):** Chair Fontes recalled a confrontational sidebar meeting where the U.S. was criticized and turned criticism into collaborative problem-solving — offered as an inspirational analogy. (Chair Fontes)
- **'80s–'90s PCS/microwave sharing; AWS/federal sharing; TSB 10F:** Mark Gibson cited these earlier sharing paradigms (culminating in guidance documents like Bulletin 10/TSB 10F) as likely precursors to new guidance documents emerging from this effort. (Mark Gibson)
- **1710–1755 auction experience:** Law-enforcement surveillance bandwidth needs were not made public before that auction; sensitivities are expected to be lower now given operational experience, though some sensitive/non-classified discussions are still anticipated. (Karl Nebbia)
- **~15 years prior to 2012 (i.e., ~1997):** Charlie Rush joked that 15 years earlier he had sworn that if he ever advocated the kind of parameter-setting exercise he was now proposing, he would know it was time to leave — underscoring his long history in the field. (Charlie Rush)
- **November (prior to May 30, 2012) Stanford working-group meeting:** Spectrum Management Improvements group had initial recommendations on the table there; revised responses to committee comments are now nearly complete. (Mark Gibson)
- **Prior to May 30, 2012:** Gary Patrick resigned as co-chair of the Search for 500 MHz committee to join the FCC. (Chair Fontes)
- **Prior 6+ months (as of May 30, 2012):** No face-to-face public-comment period had been offered at CSMAC meetings in Washington (raised by Mr. Snider); Chair Fontes characterized the May 30 meeting as informational (no reports for comment) and confirmed there is no policy to eliminate public comments going forward.

## 5. Transition Timelines (Relocation / Coexistence Periods Stated by Agencies)

| System / Use Case | Stated Timeline | Who Stated It | Key Conditions / Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1710–1755 MHz federal systems (prior relocation, mostly microwave)** | **5 years** (completed) | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Used as a baseline; simpler because systems were mostly microwave. Cost rose from ~$900M (2001 estimate) to ~$1.5B actual. |
| **Most 1755–1850 MHz federal systems (general)** | **Up to 10 years** | Karl Nebbia (NTIA), citing agencies in the Oct 1, 2011 report | Agencies can move "almost all" systems out in 10 years, except satellite uplinks and EW training which must remain. Time needed to start new projects, redesign/build systems and infrastructure, while continuing operations during conversion. Estimated ~$18 billion price tag. |
| **1755–1850 airborne systems (UAVs, PGMs, air combat training, telemetry)** | **Longer than the 5-year microwave timeline**; subsumed in the 10-year figure | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Called the "biggest challenge"; cannot be relocated without addressing complex compatibility (e.g., moving sat-uplink-type emitters near deep-space satellite receivers can blind the receivers). Outcomes depend on T-Mobile/CTIA STA measurements. |
| **Law enforcement surveillance / EOD / short-distance links — Step 1** | **5 years** from transition start | Karl Nebbia (NTIA), describing the federal law-enforcement plan in the report | Move out of 1755–1780 MHz and convert to digital; continue operating in rest of band. Relocation proceeds **city-by-city** (as in 1710–1755), not nationwide on a single date; the ordering of "NFL cities" / major markets must be coordinated with commercial buildout priorities. |
| **Law enforcement — Step 2** | "At a later date" (no firm date) | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Potentially squeeze into fewer MHz via improved digital capability; technology-dependent. |
| **Law enforcement — Step 3 (complete exit)** | Long-term / open-ended | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Potentially exit the band altogether *if* another suitable band can be identified; not guaranteed. |
| **Satellite control links & EW training ranges** | **Indefinite / "not moving within a near time frame"** | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Must remain in-place; will be protected via a regulatory construct (exclusion zones, coordination, etc.) rather than relocated. Moving sat systems to 2025–2110 MHz would require another FCC rulemaking. |
| **Fixed microwave systems** | Not separately specified; expected to be faster (experienced team from 1710–1755, led by Gary Patrick) | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Relatively easy to relocate (compared with airborne); past experience showed real-world exclusion zones shrank substantially once operators tailored deployments. |
| **Tactical radio relay** | Not separately specified; viewed as tractable | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Three permanent sites; optimism exclusion areas can be narrowed; government moving toward commercial-type tech may help create shared training opportunities. |
| **Weather satellite receivers (1695–1710 MHz)** | Process targeted for completion by **September 2012** (working-group output); operational transition timing not separately stated | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | WG will revisit exclusion-area size/locations; issues considered more limited, hence the earlier WG deadline. |
| **Commercial availability / FCC action** | **January 2013** target for CSMAC recommendations so that at least a portion of the band can be ready to pair with **2155–2180 MHz** for auction/commission action | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Lower portion (starting at 1755) *may* be made available earlier as part of a longer-term plan; however, sharing/auction distinctions may blur — possible to auction 1755 (paired with 1710 or 2155) "right away" with shared access elsewhere in the band. |
| **Microwave relocation cost evolution** | ~**11+ years** (2001 estimate → 2012 actual) | Karl Nebbia (NTIA) | Used as a caveat that current cost estimates (~$18B) will firm up through the CSEA process as the decision point nears; working groups will not be revising the numbers themselves. |
