What Is GPT-4? (Explained Like You're 5)
==========================================

Imagine a very smart robot friend who lives inside a computer.
This robot has read TONS of books, stories, web pages, and computer code —
more words than a kid could read in a thousand lifetimes. Scientists at a
place called OpenAI built this robot and named it GPT-4. It is a bigger,
smarter version of the chatbot grown-ups have been talking about.

What Can GPT-4 Do?
------------------

GPT-4 can read words AND look at pictures, like being able to listen to a
bedtime story and look at the pictures at the same time.

The scientists gave GPT-4 lots of hard tests that grown-ups take to become
lawyers, doctors, and teachers — and GPT-4 did really well!
- On the big test to become a lawyer, it scored better than 9 out of 10
  people. That is like getting an A+ on a super-hard test!
- It can answer math problems, write stories, help with homework, explain
  tricky ideas, write computer code, and speak many languages.
- If you show it a funny drawing or a photo of your homework, it can look at
  it and tell you what it sees or help solve the problem.

But GPT-4 is not perfect. Sometimes it makes things up (silly robot fibs!)
because it is just guessing the next word. It cannot really learn from your
chat the way you can, and it can make mistakes. So grown-ups should always
double-check important stuff it says.

How Did They Build It?
----------------------

Before building the BIG robot, the scientists built lots of TINY practice
robots first. By watching the tiny robots learn, they could guess how well
the BIG one would do — like tasting a tiny bite of cookie dough to know if
the whole batch will be yummy.

They also taught GPT-4 to be nice and safe. Human helpers read its answers
and said "that's good" or "that's bad," so GPT-4 learned not to help with
mean or dangerous things — like how your parents teach you not to touch the
hot stove.

Why Does It Matter?
-------------------

GPT-4 is exciting because it is one of the first computer helpers that can
talk with people in an almost-human way about almost anything. It can help
kids with homework, help doctors read papers, help coders write programs,
and help people who speak different languages talk to each other.

But because it is SO powerful, grown-ups need to be careful — making sure it
tells the truth, treats everyone fairly, and does not hurt anyone.

In short: GPT-4 is like a super-smart, well-read talking robot that can
read, write, look at pictures, and help with many hard tasks — but it is
still just a computer program, not a real person, and it still needs grown-up
supervision.
