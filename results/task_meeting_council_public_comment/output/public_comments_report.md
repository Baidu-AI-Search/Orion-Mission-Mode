## Speaker-by-Speaker Summaries

### 1. Jeraldine Williams
- **Topic / Agenda Item:** Item 16 — Zion Cemetery
- **Key Points / Concerns:**
  - Identified herself as a member of the Zion Cemetery Maintenance and Preservation Board.
  - Spoke on behalf of her great-great-grandmother, who was interred at Zion Cemetery in 1912, along with approximately 799 other souls.
  - Expressed gratitude that action is finally being taken so those interred can rest in peace.

### 2. Reva Iman
- **Topic / Agenda Item:** Item 16 — Zion Cemetery
- **Key Points / Concerns:**
  - Vice-Chair of the Zion Cemetery Maintenance and Preservation Board; former resident of Robles Park Village.
  - Emphasized that Zion Cemetery is not only a memorial but an educational asset, with plans for a genealogy center where families and visitors can research their ancestry.
  - Argued that completing the project would cement Tampa's reputation as a leading city in rediscovering and honoring these historic cemeteries, and that the project would be "history in the making."
- **Specific Requests/Demands:** Requested **$8 million in funding** to complete the Zion Cemetery project, including the memorial and the planned genealogy center.

### 3. Daryl Hych
- **Topic / Agenda Item:** Item 22 — Veterans Advisory Committee Board
- **Key Points / Concerns:**
  - Urged that the Veterans Advisory Committee be heavily (nearly 100%) populated by veterans, with possible inclusion of Gold Star family members, given Tampa and Florida's large veteran population.
  - Delivered extended remarks asserting "America is great," citing examples such as the service of African-American elected officials (Councilwoman Naya Young) and former President Barack Obama.
  - Questioned why no one in Tampa's city government or legal system has mounted a formal legal challenge to state-level DEI rollbacks, specifically referencing Resolution #568, and argued that silence on the issue reflects an "it's not my monkey, not my zoo" attitude that disproportionately leaves Black residents to fight these battles alone.
- **Specific Requests/Demands:** (1) Compose the Veterans Advisory Committee overwhelmingly with veterans. (2) Called on the City to take a legal/tenacious stand in defense of local DEI policies/resolutions.

### 4. Pam Cannella
- **Topic / Agenda Item:** Stormwater system / Hurricane preparedness (general public comment)
- **Key Points / Concerns:**
  - With two months until hurricane season, argued Tampa is unprepared and that the existing stormwater system has been neglected.
  - Asserted the City lacks contracted help for in-house stormwater crews to catch up on cleaning, pipe maintenance, and cave-in repairs, and that she was told "no funding" when she inquired.
  - Alleged a City social-media post misrepresented the facts about stormwater cleaning actually being performed.
  - Pointed out that trash remains in the AMI Hospital Pond, Zom Pond, and Swann Pond.
  - Noted the Mobility Department's own materials prescribe additional street sweepers and personnel, yet say this requires additional budgeting or a stormwater assessment fee increase — which she rejected.
  - Warned the administration will be held accountable if there is a repeat of the 2024 flooding.
- **Specific Requests/Demands:** Fund contracted stormwater maintenance, add street sweepers and personnel, and clean out the cited retention ponds — without raising the stormwater assessment fee.

### 6. Joseph Citro

### 7. Ashley Morrow
- **Topic / Agenda Item:** Sharing Tampa's Black history (general public comment — ongoing series)
- **Key Points / Concerns:**
  - Continued an ongoing series on Tampa's Black history, focusing this week on an 1877 incident in Hernando County involving Dave James (a Black man) who married a white woman (Ms. Day), the resulting mob violence/shoot-out involving men later linked to the Saxon family (founders of the KKK in Central Florida), and the role of contemporary newspapers in fomenting the narrative.
  - Referenced Arthur St. Clair (who performed the marriage) and Mary R. Tanner, a young Black woman who gave an affidavit after fleeing to Tampa and then Key West.
- **Specific Requests/Demands:** No policy or funding request; framed as an educational presentation, with a promise to continue Mary R. Tanner's story next week.

### 8. Carroll Ann Bennett
- **Topic / Agenda Item:** Sidewalk code / sidewalk construction loophole
- **Key Points / Concerns:**
  - Recounted the history of the 2020–2021 sidewalk code amendment process with Walk Bike Tampa / Sidewalk Stompers, aimed at closing the loophole that allows developers to skip sidewalk construction when "no sidewalks are nearby."
  - Said staff in 2021 promised the "no additional development nearby" exception would be used "very, very rarely," especially within a half-mile of schools, but that the exception has been used in about two-thirds of waiver cases — more than every other loophole combined.
  - Alleged the City is interpreting the "no additional development nearby" language so liberally that even sites with sidewalks on both sides of the street have been granted the exemption, and that some developers have been allowed to remove sidewalks from previously approved site plans.
  - Argued the phrase is vague and subjective (e.g., "active building permits in the area," "simultaneously" is short-term, not long-term), giving staff undue discretion.
- **Specific Requests/Demands:** Close/eliminate the "no sidewalks nearby / no additional development nearby" loophole in the sidewalk code, so developers are required to build sidewalks as originally intended.

## Thematic Summary

The 16 public speakers (14 in person, 2 online) addressed a wide range of issues before the Council. Grouping comments by topic area:

### 1. Historical Preservation & Black History — **5 speakers (highest public interest)**
- **Jeraldine Williams, Reva Iman, Robin Lockett, Adriana Rodriguez, Ashley Morrow.**
- The single most-represented topic, driven heavily by two related but distinct cemetery issues:
  - **Zion Cemetery** (Williams, Iman, Lockett): Strong support with a direct ask of **$8 million** to complete the memorial and genealogy center; repeated thanks to Council and to Richard Gonzmart for the land swap.
  - **Colon Cemetery** (Rodriguez): Urgent plea to halt development and use Council's permitting/oversight authority and Florida Statute §872 to protect another erased Black burial ground — with a direct challenge to Council to prove that promises made after Zion were not "lip service."
  - **Black History education** (Morrow): An ongoing educational series on 1870s Hernando County racial violence and Klan ties, delivered without a specific policy ask.
- **Takeaway:** Historical accountability — especially for Tampa's erased African American cemeteries — generated the broadest and most coordinated public engagement at this meeting.

### 2. Infrastructure / Stormwater / Hurricane Preparedness — **4 speakers**
- **Pam Cannella, Steve Michelini, James Adair, Stephanie Poynor.**
- Speakers criticized City stormwater maintenance ahead of hurricane season (no contracted pipe/catch-basin cleaners, trash remaining in retention ponds, repeat of 2024 flooding risk), complained that South Howard closures improperly combined stormwater and Pipes Program work without required business/homeowner notice, flagged a $200k+ emergency generator that has sat idle since 2020 while new units are being purchased for lift stations, and contrasted a proposed ~$40 million forensics lab with unmet neighborhood demand for sidewalks, streets, and stormwater fixes.
- **Takeaway:** A clear current of frustration that basic infrastructure maintenance is being neglected while headline projects and new capital purchases move forward.

### 3. Land Use, Housing & Development — **4 speakers**
- **Stephanie Poynor, Valerie Bullock, Michael Randolph, and Adriana Rodriguez (cross-listed).**
- Heavy focus on the **Rome Yard / West Tampa redevelopment**: Poynor argued the Related Group contract (1,436 days old) should be canceled rather than extended given missed deadlines and an unfulfilled Community Benefits Agreement, while Randolph (West Tampa CDC) presented the CBA vision — a Technology, Wealth Building & Job Creation Center with AI, local hiring, minority business support, and anti-displacement/anti-gentrification features.
- Bullock questioned why apartments appeared to be built before zoning approval in her Ponce de Leon/College Hill neighborhood and raised fire-safety concerns about density near Middleton High School, plus objections to Aquino Property Management.
- Rodriguez framed cemetery protection as counterweight to "unchecked development."
- **Takeaway:** West Tampa / Rome Yard is the focal point of development-accountability debate, with tension between community-benefit expectations and developer performance.

### 4. Transportation & Walkability — **2 speakers**
- **Joseph Citro (tri-county MPO, Rays stadium traffic, rail), Carroll Ann Bennett (sidewalk loophole).**
- Citro pushed for rail investment and scrutiny of the upcoming tri-county MPO structure so Tampa neighborhoods are not buried under new commuter roads, and urged early traffic planning (TPD/HCSO/FHP) around any potential Rays stadium near Dale Mabry and Tampa Bay Boulevard.
- Bennett argued the sidewalk code's "no sidewalks nearby / no additional development nearby" exception has been abused in roughly two-thirds of waiver cases — even on streets where sidewalks already exist — undermining walkability.
- **Takeaway:** Residents want less car-only planning and stronger enforcement of sidewalk requirements, especially near schools.

### 5. Policing / Public Safety — **2 speakers**
- **Tiffany Poole, Daryl Hych (partial).**
- Poole delivered an emotional account of her 15-year-old son and a 12-year-old friend being stopped and patted down by ~8 officers (vs. two listed on the report) near Bayshore, without a parent present, alleging racial bias, a false report, and resulting counseling needs for her child — and requesting body-cam footage and Council intervention.
- Hych's DEI remarks also touched on equitable treatment under the law.
- **Takeaway:** While only two speakers addressed policing, Poole's testimony was the most emotionally charged of the session and resulted in the Chair directing staff to collect her contact information for follow-up.

### 6. Veterans & City Boards — **2 speakers**
- **Daryl Hych, Stephanie Poynor** (both on Item 22, Veterans Advisory Committee).
- Both speakers (a veteran and a veteran's spouse) independently urged that the Veterans Advisory Committee be composed overwhelmingly of actual veterans, with Poynor additionally recommending a smaller board size (≤8–9 members) and criticizing the staggered-term structure.
- **Takeaway:** Clear, cross-speaker consensus that the Veterans Board should be veteran-led and streamlined.

### 7. Homelessness — **1 speaker**
- **David Moss Cornell** proposed a pilot designated gathering site on public land in Highland Pines (7401 E. Broadway, capacity ~15, Mon–Fri) with rules and enforcement.

### 8. Civic Process & City Staff Conduct — **1–2 speakers**
- **Steve Michelini** raised a personal-attack allegation against a City Communications staff member and violations of street-closure notice agreements.
- **Adriana Rodriguez** complained about Council decorum during remote public comment.
- **Pam Cannella** alleged misleading City social media about stormwater work.
- **Takeaway:** Sub-theme of trust/transparency concerns with City communications and permit coordination.

### 9. Other
- **Ashley Morrow** (Black history education series) — informational/educational.
- **Robin Lockett** also mentioned the "Yellow Jackets" community organization being made whole.

### Topics Generating the Most Public Interest
1. **Historical preservation / Black cemeteries & Black history (5 speakers)** — clearly the dominant theme, with coordinated comments on Zion funding, Colon protection, and broader Black history.
2. **Infrastructure/stormwater (4 speakers)** and **Land use/housing/development (4 speakers)** — tied for second, reflecting sustained frustration with maintenance backlogs and with major development projects (Rome Yard in particular) outpacing community benefits and accountability.
3. **Transportation/walkability (2)**, **Policing (2)**, and **Veterans board (2)** each drew multiple speakers with aligned or reinforcing asks.
