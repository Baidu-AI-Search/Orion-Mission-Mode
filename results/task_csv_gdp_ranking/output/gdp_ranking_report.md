## 6. Interpretation: A Highly Concentrated Global Economy

The 2014 global GDP distribution is a textbook example of extreme Pareto-style concentration. A **bipolar lead** — the United States (~22%) and China (~13%) — together generate more than a third of world output, with Japan, Germany, France, and the UK forming a clear but smaller second tier. The gap between the smallest G7 economy and the next country down is substantial, and the drop-off from the top 20 to the median country is dramatic: the median economy produces just over **$21 billion**, meaning half of the world's countries/territories are smaller economically than mid-sized cities in the advanced economies. At the very bottom, a long tail of microstates and small island territories collectively contributes a negligible share of world output. In short, 2014's economic map is one in which global production power is overwhelmingly concentrated in North America, Western Europe, and East Asia, while the vast majority of the world's political entities are economically very small.
