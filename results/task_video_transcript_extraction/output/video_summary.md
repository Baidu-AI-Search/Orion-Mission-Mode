## Summary

This is the official music video for Rick Astley's 1987 global hit single "Never Gonna Give You Up," written and produced by the legendary British songwriting and production trio Stock Aitken Waterman. The video features a then-21-year-old Rick Astley performing the song in various settings, including a dark brick-walled backdrop where he delivers the lead vocals, interspersed with scenes of him dancing in a beige raincoat along a riverside path, and footage of a female love interest. The song itself is an upbeat 1980s dance-pop and blue-eyed soul track built around a distinctive synthesizer bassline, electronic drum machine beat, and Astley's deep, rich baritone vocals. Lyrically, the song is a straightforward love anthem in which the singer pledges unwavering loyalty and devotion to his partner, promising he will never give her up, let her down, desert her, make her cry, say goodbye, or tell a lie to hurt her. The song was a massive commercial success upon its release, spending five weeks at number one on the UK Singles Chart in 1987 and reaching number one on the US Billboard Hot 100 in March 1988. Decades after its release, the video achieved unprecedented second life as the centerpiece of the "Rickrolling" internet meme—a bait-and-switch prank where a hyperlink purporting to be relevant content instead redirects viewers to this very video. This phenomenon, which originated on 4chan around 2007, has made the video one of the most viewed and recognizable in internet history, surpassing one billion views on YouTube and cementing its place in digital culture. Rick Astley himself has embraced the meme, making numerous surprise appearances referencing it over the years. (268 words)

## Key Points

- **1980s Pop Classic**: "Never Gonna Give You Up" was Rick Astley's debut solo single, released on July 27, 1987, and became the UK's best-selling single of that year.
- **Chart Success**: The song reached #1 in over 25 countries, including five weeks atop the UK Singles Chart and two weeks at #1 on the US Billboard Hot 100 in 1988.
- **Stock Aitken Waterman Production**: Written and produced by the hit-making trio Mike Stock, Matt Aitken, and Pete Waterman, known for their signature 1980s synth-pop sound.
- **Music Video Content**: Features Astley singing and dancing in multiple locations—dancing in a trench coat by a river/industrial area, performing in front of a brick archway, and scenes of the love interest appearing and disappearing.
- **The Rickrolling Meme**: Beginning in 2007 on 4chan, the video became the basis of one of the internet's most enduring pranks, where misleading links redirect to this video. It is considered one of the first major viral internet memes.
- **Massive YouTube Milestone**: The official upload has amassed well over one billion views, making it one of the most-watched videos from the 1980s on the platform.
- **Cultural Legacy**: The meme's popularity led to mainstream media coverage, live performances by Astley (including the 2008 Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade surprise), and even YouTube itself Rickrolling its users on April Fools' Day 2008.
- **Lyrical Theme**: A simple, sincere pledge of romantic loyalty and commitment, built around the iconic refrain of six promises (never give up, let down, desert, make cry, say goodbye, tell a lie/hurt).
