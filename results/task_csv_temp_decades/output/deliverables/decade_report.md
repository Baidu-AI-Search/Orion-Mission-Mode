## 6. Summary — Progress of Warming Decade by Decade

From the **1880s** (average anomaly -0.212 °C) to the **2010s** (+0.805 °C), GISTEMP records a net warming of roughly **+1.02 °C** in decadal-mean terms. The progression unfolds in several broad phases:

- **1880s → 1890s**: slight cooling (-0.032 °C).
- **1890s → 1900s**: slight cooling (-0.076 °C).
- **1900s → 1910s**: near-flat / plateau (-0.011 °C).
- **1910s → 1920s**: modest warming (+0.092 °C).
- **1920s → 1930s**: strong warming (+0.120 °C).
- **1930s → 1940s**: strong warming (+0.164 °C).
- **1940s → 1950s**: slight cooling (-0.092 °C).
- **1950s → 1960s**: near-flat / plateau (+0.017 °C).
- **1960s → 1970s**: modest warming (+0.064 °C).
- **1970s → 1980s**: strong warming (+0.211 °C).
- **1980s → 1990s**: strong warming (+0.138 °C).
- **1990s → 2000s**: strong warming (+0.204 °C).
- **2000s → 2010s**: strong warming (+0.217 °C) — the single largest step-up in the entire record.
- **2010s → 2020s**: strong warming (+0.175 °C).

Key takeaways:

- Warming was **uneven through the early 20th century**, with the 1880s–1910s hovering in a relatively cool band around −0.3 to −0.4 °C.
- A clear early-20th-century warming pulse lifted anomalies through the **1930s/1940s**, followed by a mid-century plateau (and slight dip in the 1950s–1970s) often associated with aerosol cooling.
- From the **1980s onward**, every single decade has been warmer than the one before it, and the pace of warming has accelerated sharply.
- The **2000s→2010s** transition produced the largest single-decade jump (+0.217 °C), and the 2010s are the warmest full decade on record.
- The **partial 2020s** (2020–2023) average +0.980 °C, already substantially above the 2010s mean; if the decade completed on this trajectory it would set yet another record.
- Variability has been modest (typical intra-decade σ ≈ 0.08–0.14 °C), with the **2010s** the noisiest and the **1920s** the steadiest — the recent decades' warming signal is therefore not a product of a single outlier year but a robust shift of the entire distribution upward.
