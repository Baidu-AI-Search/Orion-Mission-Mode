# Next Steps, Follow-Up Actions & Deliverables

**Source:** NASA's First Public Meeting on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs) — UAP Independent Study Team Public Meeting
**Transcript date:** May 31, 2023 (meeting day)
**Compiled from:** Transcript of deliberations, expert presentations, and public Q&A

> The panel was a deliberative working session. As Dan Evans noted at the opening, the team still had "several months of work ahead of them" when this meeting was held, and the items below reflect planned and in-progress actions announced during the proceedings, as well as open questions raised but not yet assigned.

## Open Questions (raised but without a clear follow-up assignment)

These topics were raised during discussion but no explicit owner, deliverable, or timeline was assigned. They are candidates for the panel's final report recommendations.

- **Q1. Raw vs. processed FAA radar data retention.** Reggie Brothers asked whether FAA could retain raw (unprocessed) radar data to enable post-hoc calibration and phenomenology capture. Mike Freie noted FAA currently retains processed displays (ERAM/STAR) on a legal/accident-investigation timeline measured in months; no commitment was made on raw retention. *(Owner: TBD — FAA / potential panel recommendation)*
- **Q2. Synthetic data for AI/ML training.** Speaker 4 raised generating synthetic UAP data to train ML models given sparse real data. Federica Bianco flagged bias risk; no decision or owner was assigned. *(Owner: TBD — AARO / research community)*
- **Q3. Definition of "anomalous" / target phenomenology.** Mike Gold pressed repeatedly on *what* signature (radiation? electromagnetic? acceleration?) NASA should be looking for. Sean Kirkpatrick provided AARO's working definition, but the panel acknowledged the scientific community does not yet have a settled phenomenological target list. *(Owner: Panel / NASA / AARO — ongoing)*
- **Q4. How to handle eyewitness reports quantitatively.** Panel agreed eyewitness accounts alone are insufficient but may contain useful spatial/seasonal information; how to couple them with sensor data and with biophysical/psychophysical witness assessments is not yet designed. *(Owner: TBD — recommended for roadmap)*
- **Q5. Stigma inside government buildings and leadership.** Sean Kirkpatrick and Mike Gold both asserted stigma persists at senior leadership levels across agencies; solutions beyond NASA's brand leadership were not specified. *(Owner: Whole-of-government)*
- **Q6. Extent of NASA current-asset relevance.** Shelley Wright noted no analysis had yet been done mapping NASA sensors to AARO's target characterization; the proposed task force (N-10) is a recommendation, not a chartered activity. *(Owner: NASA — pending)*
- **Q7. Privacy/legal constraints on pointing large DOD/IC collection at domestic locations.** Kirkpatrick flagged the legal trade-space; no policy solution was proposed. *(Owner: DOD/IC/DOJ/Congress)*
- **Q8. Budget level for a future NASA UAP program.** Dan Evans explicitly said no programmatic funding existed yet; figures contingent on the report and federal budget process. *(Owner: NASA / Congress)*
- **Q9. Whether/how to modify existing ADS-B / cooperative-surveillance and non-cooperative radar filters to retain rather than discard non-aircraft targets.** Raised in the context of FAA filter optimization removing leaves, insects, drones, etc.; no commitment given. *(Owner: FAA / potential recommendation)*
- **Q10. Crowdsourcing platform governance and design.** Agreed that "a set of guidelines" is insufficient and a real platform is needed, but who builds, hosts, moderates, curates, and funds it was not decided. *(Owner: NASA / TBD partners)*
