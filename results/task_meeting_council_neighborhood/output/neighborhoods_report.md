## 6. Notes & Caveats

- The city's official disclaimer states this file is an **unedited realtime caption** and may contain errors/omissions. Location spellings follow the transcript verbatim; clear capitalization/word-boundary variants (e.g. "TPA" = Tampa International Airport; "District Five" = District 5) are normalized to a single canonical form in the frequency table.
- District assignments for neighborhoods are best-effort based on the **2023 Tampa City Council redistricting map**. Several neighborhoods (e.g., Seminole Heights, Riverside Heights, Robles Park) sit near district boundaries and are tagged accordingly (e.g. "District 1/5"). Explicit district references from speakers (e.g. "District 1", "District 3") are tagged to the district named even when the location differs from the canonical map (for example, the speaker who described being on Bayshore in "District 3" is captured verbatim even though Bayshore is generally mapped to District 4).
- Specific addresses and intersections that appear verbatim in testimony (e.g., 4102 N 22nd St, 7401 E Broadway Ave, 50th and Busch, South Howard, 34th Street) are included as location-level entries, even where the speaker did not name the surrounding neighborhood.
- Named stormwater ponds (Swann Pond, AMI Hospital Pond, Zom Pond), named parks (Gandy Park South, A.J. Palonis Park), and named facilities (Howard Avenue Annex, Rick's on the River, Blake HS, Middleton HS, Station 13, Crockett's Towing) are treated as specific locations because they were the subject of public-works or public-safety testimony.
- References to neighboring counties/cities (Pinellas, St. Petersburg/Tropicana, Polk, Pasco, Hernando/Brooksville, Sarasota, Key West, eastern Hillsborough County) and out-of-state biographical references (San Jose State University) are catalogued because council members and speakers explicitly invoked them to frame transportation, historical, or stadium-related debate.
- Very generic terms such as "the city," "here," "downtown" without additional anchor context are only counted when they appear as part of a proper-name string (e.g., "Downtown Tampa") or when "Downtown" is used as a proper noun in historical/development discussion (e.g., Charlie Miranda's recollections of Skid Row, the Straz, the Tampa Theatre, Riverwalk).
