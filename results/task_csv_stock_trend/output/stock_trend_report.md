## 1. Overall Trend Direction: **Strongly Bullish**

Apple shares posted a powerful uptrend throughout 2014, delivering one of the strongest yearly gains in the company's recent history. The price chart shows the classic profile of a secular advance: an early-year shakeout, followed by two long, stair-step rallies separated by a brief autumn pause. Buying pressure dominated the tape, with 129 up days versus 110 down days (53.8% vs. 46.2%).

## 4. Longest Consecutive Streaks

### Longest winning streak (consecutive up days)
- **Length:** **9 trading days**
- **Dates:** **August 11, 2014 → August 21, 2014**
- **Price move:** $94.49 → $99.74 (**+5.6%**)
- **Context:** This streak occurred in the heart of the summer rally, just ahead of Apple's September 9 iPhone 6/Apple Watch event, as anticipation built for the company's largest product cycle in years.

### Longest losing streak (consecutive down days)
- **Length:** **5 trading days**
- **Dates:** **January 27, 2014 → January 31, 2014**
- **Price move:** $76.66 → $69.00 (**−10.0%**)
- **Context:** This five-day drop capped the broader January sell-off, taking AAPL to its 2014 low of $69.00. The sell-off was driven by concerns over iPhone demand in China and a broad emerging-market/ risk-off episode. It was the deepest pullback of the entire year and established the low from which the rest of 2014's bull market launched.

> Note: Down streaks (max 5 days) were materially shorter than up streaks (max 9 days), which is characteristic of a strong uptrend — buyers quickly stepped in on dips.

## 5. Sustained Movement Periods

Beyond the single-day streaks, several multi-week moves defined 2014:

### 🔴 January Sell-off — *January 2 → January 31* (18 trading days, **−9.0%**)
- $75.86 → $69.00
- The year opened with a sharp, fast pullback. After tagging the January 31 low at $69.00 (the year's absolute low), the stock bottomed and never looked back for the rest of 2014. This was the only meaningful correction of H1.

### 🟢 February Recovery — *January 28 → February 18* (15 trading days, **+8.0%**)
- $70.90 → $76.55
- A classic V-shaped bounce off the lows, recovering roughly 90% of January's decline by mid-February. This bounce confirmed that buyers were defending the $70 level aggressively.

### 🟢 Spring-to-Summer Mega-Rally — *March 27 → September 3* (111 trading days, **+35.1%**)
- $75.71 → $102.26
- The **dominant trend of the year**: a relentless five-month uptrend that carried AAPL from the mid-$70s above $100 for the first time post-split. The move was fueled by strong iPhone/iPad earnings, the announced 7-for-1 stock split (effective June 9), growing capital return program (dividends + buybacks), and anticipation of the iPhone 6 launch. There was no pullback greater than ~8% during this entire stretch — a textbook "buy-the-dip" trend.

### 🟡 September Pause — *September 3 → October 15* (~28 trading days)
- $102.26 → ~$94.77 (≈ −7.3% from the Sep 3 peak to the Oct 15 intraday low)
- A shallow, healthy consolidation following the big summer run. The "sell-the-news" reaction after the September 9 iPhone 6 / Apple Watch event, compounded by the mid-October broad-market Ebola/global-growth scare, provided the last notable entry point before the final push higher.

### 🟢 Q4 Rally — *October 10 → November 28* (35 trading days, **+19.0%**)
- $99.86 → $118.80
- A powerful post-correction surge driven by blockbuster iPhone 6/6 Plus sales (the "bigger screen" super-cycle), strong Q4 earnings, and the all-time high print of $118.80 on **Black Friday, November 28**.
- This was the sharpest velocity move of the year, with the stock gaining nearly a fifth of its value in under two months.

### 🔴 December Pullback (partial month) — *Nov 28 → Dec 12*
- $118.80 → $110.03 (≈ −7.4%)
- Profit-taking into year-end after the November peak. Given that the dataset ends December 12, we do not observe the full month's resolution, but the pullback was modest in context of the year's gains.

## 6. Summary

**2014 was a decisively bullish year for Apple shares**, with the stock appreciating roughly **42%** from the first trading day to the last day in the dataset, and reaching a gain of more than **72%** from the January low to the November high.

The year's price action can be summarized in four acts:
1. **January shakeout** (−9%) — a brief but sharp sell-off established the year's low at $69.
2. **Spring–Summer mega-rally** (+35% over ~5 months) — a steady, almost uninterrupted climb past the 7-for-1 split in June and above $100 for the first time post-split.
3. **Autumn pause** (~−7% in Sep–Oct) — a shallow, healthy consolidation into the iPhone 6 launch and the October market scare.
4. **Q4 surge** (+19% in ~7 weeks) — an explosive rally to a new all-time high of $118.80 on November 28, followed by mild year-end profit-taking.

Bullish structural characteristics — nine consecutive up days in August, only five consecutive down days at the worst point in January, 8-of-9 positive full months, and a monthly average price that climbed every month from February through August — all confirm that **buyers were in control throughout 2014**. The primary catalysts were the massively anticipated iPhone 6/6 Plus launch (Apple's first large-screen phones, which ignited a record upgrade cycle), the 7-for-1 stock split in June, and continued expansion of Apple's capital return program. By year-end 2014, AAPL had firmly re-established itself as one of the strongest large-cap momentum stocks in the market.
