## Key Topics Discussed

1. **Corporate Events Support** — The team reviewed the current model where each Go-To-Market (GTM) team "sponsors" a corporate event, with the PMM and campaign manager owning that event's support. Event-to-team assignments were validated, and the group discussed how to lock in commitments from campaign managers.

2. **GitLab Commit Product Announcements** — The team worked through how to surface meaningful, press-worthy announcements for the upcoming Commit event (and the product/Sid keynotes). Because GitLab ships public MVPs continuously, the approach is to look back over the past ~12 months and identify features that have matured from MVP to GA-ready, grouped into thematic buckets (e.g., vulnerability management, UX improvements, GitOps). The goal is ~3 items per stage, with a "Top 5" overall for PR.

3. **Competitive Comparison / Battlecards** — Samia raised questions about the competitor-per-stage spreadsheet (currently showing the same five tier-1 competitors copied across all tabs). The team reviewed the new design-team infographic and agreed on a "market lens" approach (not GitLab-only features) and a green-only color scheme to keep the asset feeling like a helpful industry comparison rather than a "shill" piece.

4. **Core Messaging Framework** — William walked through draft positioning pillars and the group refined phrasing for three messaging buckets (transparency/single source of truth, end-to-end control, and speed-with-confidence), debating tone, parallelism, and pithiness.

## Decisions Made

- **Event assignments confirmed:** Platform → re:Invent; CI/CD → Google Next; GitOps → KubeCon.
- **Commit announcements strategy:** Bundle incremental MVPs into themed stories rather than announcing individual small features; reuse GitLab 14.0 content and add notable additions (e.g., VS Code community extensions becoming official, Terraform module). Tentative top-5 themes include UX improvements, Vulnerability Management, and GitOps (Kubernetes agent + HashiCorp integrations). Excitement ratings (1–3) will be used as a stack-rank aid, informed by MAUs, upvotes, and customer anecdotes.
- **Competitive asset scope:** Only tier-1 competitors that actually apply to each stage will be filled in; gaps (e.g., platform vendors with zero monitoring capabilities) will be explicitly called out; a future iteration will add drill-down feature descriptions; green-only palette retained for V1.
- **Messaging pillars directionally locked:** "A single source of truth, countless possibilities," "End-to-end control over your software factory," and "More speed, less risk" emerged as the preferred phrases.
