## What to Watch

- **April 16 is a "mega-meeting."** It will see the return of two of the meeting's most contested items — the **Veterans Affairs Committee ordinance** (with amendments on membership size, Gold Star cap, and term length still being negotiated among Council members) and the **TPD Howard Avenue Annex / Forensics Lab resolutions** (Items 26–28, $34.2M, requiring a second reading per Council rules for contracts over $20M). It also brings forward the $650K park-funding reallocation and the 2027 budget-workshop times.
- **Rome Yard / West River – watch the delivery, not just the vote.** Council approved the Phase 4 extension to September 2028 by only 5-2 (Carlson & Clendenin "no"), with sharp public criticism over repeated extensions, CBA timing, and alleged preferential treatment. Key things to track: (1) the promised 60-day financial close and construction start on 230 affordable units; (2) quarterly reports that now must include **both** CBA compliance and overall construction progress; (3) whether future phases (1, 2, and townhomes) come back individually for more extensions (staff warned this is likely); and (4) delivery of the minority-business list Councilwoman Young requested.
- **TFR Fire Station No. 24 is quietly running ahead of schedule.** Groundbreaking is now targeted **mid-to-late August 2026** (a month early), GMP delivery pulled forward to **May 15** (from July 26), Council GMP approval expected **late June**, and substantial completion now **September 1, 2027** (from October 22). With TFR No. 25 and the Howard Avenue Annex also advancing, this is one of the few capital projects drawing cross-Council praise — watch for the GMP vote in June.
- **Ethics & transparency threads to monitor.** (1) The **May 7 legal report** on whether CDC/Housing Authority employees who serve on CACs can apply for CRA grants — Councilwoman Young wants the Attorney's Office, not CRA staff, to set the rule publicly. (2) Councilman Carlson's standing reminder that any **binding Tampa Bay Rays MOU** must get two readings over two weeks. (3) The call for **uniform SOPs for all boards/committees** (attendance rules, terms, appointments), with a volunteer to be recruited by April 16.
- **Land-use and cemetery controversies.** The **4102 N. 22nd Street rezoning** (REZ 25-126) passed unanimously, but staff committed to a site inspection after residents pointed to apparent pre-approval construction — watch what inspectors find. **Colon Cemetery** got no formal motion but drew emotional testimony invoking the Zion Cemetery precedent; watch for any pause on permits or additional land-swap efforts. And on the county side, **HC CPA 25-37** (up to ~4,000 units in eastern Hillsborough) was flagged as a potential threat to Tampa's water supply — expect continued regional pressure.
- **Water/wastewater capacity fees start their four-year climb.** First reading of the water/wastewater capacity-fee increase passed (with Councilman Carlson the lone "no" on Item 25). Watch for the second reading and for how the new fees — phased in over four years under Florida Statute limits — affect new development and the $1.8B five-year water/wastewater CIP; public comment flagged roughly $1.8M–$2M in foregone capacity fees over recent years.
- **Quick-hit items:** (1) **April 9 Budget Advisory Committee** welcomes Tarah Bluma (Carlson's new appointee). (2) **April 20 East Tampa Town Hall** at Fair Oaks, 6:30 P.M. (3) The **Marion Street transfer back from HART** to the City — Chair Clendenin signaled he will make the motion at the next HART board meeting, but Councilman Carlson flagged that Council itself has not yet voted on it. (4) **3–4 new All-Abilities interns** start in the next 1–2 weeks. (5) The unused **2020 mobile generator** — a public crowdsourcing effort worth watching for a deployment decision before hurricane season.
